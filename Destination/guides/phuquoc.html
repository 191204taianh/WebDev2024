Phu Quoc
Phu Quoc City, situated in the Gulf of Thailand, is a stunning island destination off the southwestern coast of Vietnam. Renowned for its pristine beaches, lush tropical landscapes, and vibrant marine life, Phu Quoc is a haven for nature enthusiasts and beach lovers alike. With an area of approximately 574 square kilometers, it is the largest island in Vietnam. The city boasts a rich history, with influences from various cultures, including Vietnamese, Cambodian, and Chinese. Visitors can explore historic sites such as the Phu Quoc Prison, which sheds light on the island's tumultuous past. Phu Quoc is also famous for its thriving culinary scene, offering delectable seafood dishes and traditional Vietnamese cuisine. Adventure seekers can indulge in a range of activities, including snorkeling, scuba diving, hiking in the island's national parks, or simply relaxing on its pristine shores.
